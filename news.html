Feb 7–16, 2026 · Milano Cortina, Italy
Six U.S. Ski Jumpers Compete at 2026 Milano Cortina Olympic Winter Games
The United States fielded its strongest Olympic ski jumping team in years, with Kevin Bickner, Jason Colby, and Tate Frantz on the men's side and Annika Belshaw, Josie Johnson, and Paige Jones representing the women. Frantz delivered standout performances with a 21st on the normal hill and 19th on the large hill — the best results ever by a 20-year-old American Olympic ski jumper. The mixed team earned two 8th-place finishes.

Women's Program Breakthroughs

Historic
Jan 2026 · Ljubno, Slovenia
Belshaw Cracks Top 10 — First U.S. Woman Since 2017
Annika Belshaw jumped to 7th place at the Ljubno World Cup, becoming the first American woman to crack the top 10 since the 2016–17 season. She opened the season by winning her eighth national title.

Career Best
Jan 2026 · Ljubno, Slovenia
Paige Jones Jumps to Career-Best 14th — Third Improvement This Season
19-year-old Paige Jones secured her first-ever top-15 finish at Ljubno, marking the third time this season she's improved her career-best World Cup result.
